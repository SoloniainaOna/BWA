Appleton School — Internal Reference
Presenting
the environment
This guide is for the person running the presentation. It covers setup, slide-by-slide talking points, navigation controls, and what to do if something goes wrong. Keep it open on a separate device during the presentation.
1
Before You Start

Run through this checklist before the room fills in.

  • Open index.html in a browser on the presentation computer. Use Chrome or Edge for best compatibility.
  • Extend or mirror the display to the projector. Make sure the browser window is on the projected screen, set to full screen (press F11 or use the browser menu).
  • The presentation starts on Slide 01 automatically. Do not refresh mid-session — this resets to Slide 01.
  • Keep this guide open on a separate device (phone, tablet, or a second monitor not visible to the audience).
  • Silence all notifications on the presentation computer before starting.
2
Navigation Controls

The presentation is controlled entirely from the keyboard or mouse. There is no auto-advance.

Action Input
Next slide → ↓ Space or click the right half of the screen
Previous slide ← ↑ or click the left half of the screen
Go to first slide Refresh the page (F5)
Full screen F11 (Windows) or browser full screen
Exit full screen F11 or Esc
ℹ
Tip The current slide number is always shown in the top-right corner of the screen, and progress dots are visible at the bottom. The audience cannot see these clearly from a distance — they are for your reference.
3
Slide-by-Slide Reference
Slide 01 — Welcome
Welcome to Appleton
This is the opening slide. It sets the tone and introduces the school's digital art environment. Hold here while the audience settles — it is designed to stand on its own visually.
  • Introduce yourself and the purpose of the presentation.
  • Mention that what you're about to show is a digital environment installed in the school — not a website or an app, but a physical space with interactive digital works.
  • The BWA platform is what powers these experiences.
Slide 02 — The Environment
Three Experiences, One Space
This slide gives an overview of all three experiences. It is the map before the journey. Use it to frame what the audience is about to see in detail.
  • There are three distinct interactive works, each exploring a different way of engaging with a moving image.
  • Students don't just watch — they touch, divide, reshape, and reconstruct.
  • The three cards on screen are the three experiences you will present next, one by one.
Slide 03 — Experience 01
Ripple & Trail
First of the three experience detail slides. Focuses on the water-ripple interactive artwork. The tagline "Motion as a brush" captures the core idea.
  • The video surface responds to touch or cursor movement in real time.
  • Each gesture leaves a trail that fades naturally — no two interactions are the same.
  • The physics are based on water wave propagation: rings expand outward, overlap, and decay.
  • Built on WebGL shaders — it runs entirely in the browser with no installation needed.
Slide 04 — Experience 02
Divide & Filters
Second experience detail slide. The student becomes a compositor — splitting the image and assigning different filters to each region.
  • Students drag across the screen to draw dividing lines. Each region is independent.
  • A double tap on any region cycles through 8 filters: Normal, Black & White, Pink, Sepia, Negative, Cold, Contrast, Vintage.
  • The result is a live artwork — made from the same video, but seen differently in every section.
  • There is no right answer. Every student's composition is unique.
Slide 05 — Experience 03
Puzzle
Third experience detail slide. The most game-like of the three — familiar enough to be immediately approachable, surprising in that the pieces contain live video, not a static image.
  • The video is cut into pieces that students physically rearrange on screen.
  • Each piece is not a frozen screenshot — it plays the video live within its bounds.
  • A small preview plays in the corner so students always have a reference for where each piece belongs.
  • Four difficulty levels — from 8 pieces to 36 — so the experience scales for different ages and abilities.
Slide 06 — Closing
Art, Technology and Curiosity
The closing slide brings the three experiences back together under a single message. Hold here for questions or discussion.
  • These three works are not standalone apps — they are part of a unified environment installed in the school.
  • The intention is to give students a hands-on relationship with art and technology, not just a passive one.
  • Open the floor to questions here.

Troubleshooting
⚠
Slides are not advancing Make sure the browser window is in focus — click once on the presentation screen before using keyboard shortcuts. The window must be active to receive key input.
⚠
Presentation appears zoomed in or cut off on the projector Open browser zoom settings and set to 100% (Ctrl + 0). Then enter full screen mode with F11. The presentation is designed for a 16:9 aspect ratio.
⚠
Fonts are not loading (text looks wrong) The presentation loads fonts from Google Fonts and requires an internet connection. If the room has no internet, the fallback fonts (serif / sans-serif) will be used — the layout will still work correctly.
⚠
The screen went to sleep mid-presentation Before starting, go to your computer's display settings and disable sleep / screen timeout. On Windows: Settings → System → Power → Screen timeout → Never.
⚠
Need to restart from the beginning Refresh the browser page (F5). The presentation always reloads on Slide 01.
