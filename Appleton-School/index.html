Experience 01
Ripple
& Trail
Motion as a brush
A live video becomes a reactive surface. As students move across it — by touch or by cursor — their movement leaves flowing trails of light and expanding ripple waves that propagate outward like water disturbed by a stone.

Every gesture is unique. Every path drawn disappears slowly, leaving only the memory of motion in the artwork.
  • —Continuous trail with Gaussian falloff — smooth, organic edges
  • —Expanding concentric ring waves on each touch point
  • —Click-generated ripples with natural amplitude decay
  • —Real-time WebGL rendering via Three.js shader pipeline
  • —Works with both mouse and multi-touch input

Experience 02
Divide
& Filters
One image, many identities
Students drag across the screen to divide a living image into segments. Each region becomes independent — a double tap cycles through eight distinct visual filters, transforming its color, tone, and mood.

The result is a composition entirely their own: part editorial, part painting, assembled live from a single moving source.
  • —Drag to divide the video into custom segments
  • —Double-tap any region to cycle through 8 filters
  • —Filters: Normal · B&W · Pink · Sepia · Negative · Cold · Contrast · Vintage
  • —Region isolation using flood-fill algorithm + WebGL shader rendering
  • —Choose from a selection of videos before starting

Experience 03
Puzzle
Reconstruct the moving image
The video is shattered into pieces. Students pick them up and place them back — dragging, repositioning, assembling — until the image plays whole again.

A live preview plays in the corner as a reference. Four levels of difficulty scale the challenge, from 8 to 36 pieces, making the experience accessible to every level of engagement.
  • —Drag-and-drop puzzle pieces drawn from a live video stream
  • —Snap-to-grid mechanic with 30% proximity threshold
  • —Easy: 8 pieces — Medium: 16 — Hard: 24 — Extreme: 36
  • —Live preview video in the corner for reference
  • —Choose from a selection of videos before starting
